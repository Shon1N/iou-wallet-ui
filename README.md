Core Concept 

Track who owes whom, how much, and on what terms—making personal loans between friends, family, and trusted contacts as organised as banking transactions without the bureaucracy.

Key Features 

Relationship Management 

Beneficiary System: 
Safely add trusted contacts (people/companies) as transaction partners 
Dual Verification: Both parties must accept before active lending begins 
Relationship Types: Distinguish between personal connections and business entities 

Smart Lending 

Flexible Terms: Set custom amounts, interest rates, and repayment periods 
Clear Direction: Always know who's the lender (owed money) and who's the borrower (owes money) 
Status Tracking: Real-time monitoring of loan lifecycle (Active → Settled → Closed) 

Financial Tracking 

Automated Repayment Logging: Track every payment made toward a loan 
Credit Loss Accounting: Record and analyse defaults or forgiven debts 
Balance Calculations: Automatic remaining balance and interest computations 

Risk Management 

Active/Inactive Controls: Temporarily pause relationships when needed 
Validation Rules: Prevent self-lending, duplicate relationships, and invalid transactions
Audit Trail: Complete history of all financial interactions 

Real-World Use Cases 

Personal Use 

Friends/Family Loans: "I'll lend you $500 for car repairs, pay me back in 3 months" 
Shared Expenses: Tracking who paid for group vacations or dinners 
Rent Advances: Roommates covering each other's rent

Small Business 

Vendor Credits: "Take the inventory now, pay me in 30 days" 
Client Advances: Fronting costs for trusted clients 
Partner Investments: Informal capital contributions between business partners
